The Story

Nourish Your Soul and Your Bottom Line

Once upon a time ...the world was connected

And every day … people said hello to their neighbors as they came home from work and brought their favorite dish to the block party. When someone was sick, neighbors brought soup. When someone new moved in, they were welcomed to the neighborhood. We celebrated our lives and our community.

Until one day… we moved from the neighborhood, started to build a life of our own and got busy, long hours and no reason to meet.

Because of that … we don’t know our neighbors. We don’t bring them soup. We don’t know that William’s mother is too sick to cook. We don’t know that he will go hungry tonight.

And because of that… we feel a bit lost. We feel like strangers in our neighborhood. We want to help but how? We feel stuck.

Until one day … Jenny noticed overhead the owner of her favorite sandwich shop shaking his head because he was throwing away leftover food.

And ever since that day… Jenny has been building an app to connect lost food with lost people. Donors snap a photo and post an alert that nearby drivers catch and deliver.

And the moral is we helps ourselves when we help each other. Entrepreneurs give neighbors have a reason to say hello and William gets a warm meal.

Food is symbolic of love when words are inadequate - Alan D. Wolfelt
